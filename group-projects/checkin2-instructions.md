# Check-in 2: Half PAP

This second check-in builds on the first to get you halfway there on building a PAP for your study. 

It should have the following components. The components below do *not* have to correspond to specific sections in your final document. They correspond to the content that should be covered; you are free to organize the document however makes the most sense for your particular study. You should rely on the examples shared on Canvas, and other PAPs from your field of study, to decide how to organize your PAP sections.

- Introduction
    - Background
    - Motivation
- Experimental Design
    - Intervention/Manipulation studied
    - Enrollment criteria/Sampling strategy
    - Assignment protocol/Identification strategy
- Data
    - Inputs, outputs and outcomes of interest
    - Data collection strategy, data sources
    - Hypotheses and outcomes (data construction)
- Power calculations (setup)
- Bibliography


# For reference: Full PAP

For reference, a full PAP will contain

- The same components as above, updated with our feedback after check-in 2
- Empirical strategy
- Power calculations (final)
- Risks and limitations
    - Internal validity threats
    - External validity threats
    - Attrition, spillovers, other threats
- Conclusion
- Abstract
- Bibliography
