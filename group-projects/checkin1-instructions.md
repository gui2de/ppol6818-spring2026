# Check-in 1: Topic + Theory of Change

## Overview

This first check-in establishes the foundation for your research project. You should have a clear topic, a well-defined research question, and a preliminary theory of change that explains *why* your intervention should work.

**Goal:** By the end of this check-in, your group should be able to clearly articulate what you want to study and why it matters.

---

## Required Components

### 1. Topic & Motivation (1/2 page)

**What to include:**
- What policy area or problem are you addressing?
- Why does this matter? (Policy relevance, affected population, magnitude of problem)
- What is the geographic/institutional context?

**Guiding questions:**
- Who is affected by this problem?
- What are the current approaches to addressing it?
- Why is new evidence needed?

### 2. Research Question (1/4 page)

**What to include:**
- State your research question clearly in one sentence
- Identify your key variables:
- **Treatment/Exposure (X):** What is varying?
- **Outcome (Y):** What are you measuring?
- **Population:** Who are you studying?


### 3. Theory of Change (1 page)

**What to include:**
(include at least one of these)
#### A. The Market Failure / Problem
- What is the underlying market failure or behavioral barrier?
- Why doesn't the optimal outcome occur naturally?
- Examples: information asymmetry, externalities, credit constraints, behavioral biases

#### B. The Causal Chain
- Draw a simple DAG or logic model showing:
- Intervention → Intermediate outcomes → Final outcomes
- Identify key assumptions at each step
- What mechanisms link your intervention to outcomes?

#### C. Theory of Change Diagram
Include a visual representation:

```
[Inputs] → [Activities] → [Outputs] → [Outcomes] → [Impact]
↓ ↓ ↓ ↓ ↓
Resources What you Direct Changes in Long-term
needed do products behavior effects
```

**Guiding questions:**
- Why would your intervention work?
- What assumptions must hold for your theory to be correct?
- What could break the causal chain?
- What is your counterfactual? What would happen without the intervention?
- Are there obvious differences between groups that could confound your results? (for non-RCT questions)

### 5. Initial Literature Scan (1/2 page)

**What to include:**
- 3-5 relevant papers that inform your project
- Brief description of what each paper contributes
- What gap does your project fill?

**You don't need a full lit review yet**, but you should know:
- What has been studied before?
- What do we already know?
- What questions remain?

---

## Submission Format

- **Length:** 2-3 pages (excluding references and diagrams)
- **Format:** PDF
- **Include:** All group member names
- **Diagrams:** Include at least one visual (DAG, Theory of Change diagram, or logic model)
